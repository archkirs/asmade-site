The human role is not always visible

AI may be part of the process. That alone doesn't explain what you did.

One person may use AI only to explore ideas. Another may use it to create source images that they then select, edit, and assemble manually. A third may use AI for text, translation, or research. Another may use AI across several stages of the process.

The final result doesn't always make those differences clear. A MADE Record helps show what you did and how AI was used in one specific work.

MADE Record

Brings the key context around one specific work into one place, so readers do not have to piece the process together from scattered files, chats, and versions.

Context for other people

When someone needs to understand how the work was created.

A MADE Record may be useful when a client, editor, publisher, employer, platform, contest organiser, professional community, or audience needs clearer context about how a work was created.

A MADE Record does not tell them how to judge the work. It gives them more information for their own evaluation.

How it works

From one creative work to a clearer picture of how it was made.

1

Identify the specific work

Each MADE Record is linked to a defined creative work and version.

2

Review available information

Available drafts, source files, versions, notes, AI chats, alternatives, references, and other materials may be reviewed.

3

Create the MADE Record

AsMade brings together what the creator did, how AI was used, key actions and decisions, available materials, and important limitations.

4

The creator reviews their information

The creator can review their statements and other factual details before the Record is published or shared.

5

Readers review and decide

Readers can start with the main picture, then explore the process and available materials in more detail when needed. The final judgment remains theirs.

More about the structure

MADE helps organise information about the creative process.

MADEWhat it covers
MaterialsThe final work, sketches, drafts, versions, source files, notes, references, AI chats, and other available materials
ActionsDeveloping ideas, using AI, creating, selecting, editing, assembling, checking, and revising
DecisionsCreative direction, what was selected, changed or rejected, and the choices that shaped the final work
EvolutionHow the work changed from early ideas to the final result, when that history is available

MADE is not a rigid template with four required, equally detailed parts. It is a flexible way to organise information about how a work was created.

More context, not a ready-made verdict

Not every statement in a MADE Record is supported in the same way.

A MADE Record can distinguish between:

Creator-provided information

Information provided by the creator about the work and process.

Information linked to materials

Statements linked to drafts, files, versions, AI chats, notes, or other available materials.

Directly observable information

Information visible in files or the available work history.

Findings from analysis

Conclusions drawn from the available information.

Unresolved questions

Information that is incomplete, uncertain, disputed, or cannot be established.

Missing material does not, by itself, mean someone is trying to mislead others.

What a MADE Record does not do

More context. Not proof of authorship.

A MADE Record is not an AI detector or a legal certificate. It does not prove authorship, copyright, or ownership, and it does not guarantee that every process material was preserved.

Its purpose is to make the creator's role, AI use, and available information about the process easier to understand. Questions of rights, acceptability, quality, or value are decided separately.
