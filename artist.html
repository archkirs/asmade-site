The human role is not always visible in the finished result

AI may be part of creating the work. That still does not explain what the person did.

One person may use AI only to explore ideas. Another may use it to create source images that they then select, change, and assemble manually. A third may use AI for text, translation, or research. Someone else may use it much more deeply.

The final result does not always make those differences clear. A MADE Record helps show what role the person played and what role AI played in one specific work.

Your role in creating the work

Human actions

AI use

Evolution of the work

Finished work

AI chat

MADE Record

Connects the important parts around one specific work and helps show the overall picture without asking someone to reconstruct it from every file, chat, and version.

Context for other people

When someone needs to understand how the work was created.

A MADE Record may be useful when the creation process matters to a client, editor, publisher, employer, platform, contest, professional community, audience, or another person who wants to better understand where the work came from.

A MADE Record does not decide how that person should judge the work. It gives them additional information for their own evaluation.

How it works

From a specific creative work to clearer context.

1

Identify the specific work

The MADE Record is linked to a defined creative work and version.

2

Review available information

Drafts, source files, versions, notes, AI chats, alternatives, references, and other materials may be reviewed when available.

3

Create the MADE Record

AsMade brings together the main human and AI roles, important actions and decisions, available materials, and significant limitations.

4

The creator checks their information

The creator can check their own statements and factual context before the Record is published or shared.

5

Other people review and decide

Readers can start with the main picture and go deeper into process and available materials when needed.

More about the structure

MADE helps organise information about the creative process.

MADECreative-work context
MaterialsThe final work, sketches, drafts, versions, source files, notes, references, AI chats, and other available materials
ActionsDeveloping ideas, using AI, creating, selecting, editing, assembling, checking, and revising
DecisionsCreative direction, selection, rejection of weak results, changes, and final choices
EvolutionHow the work changed from early ideas to the final result, when that history is available

MADE does not mean that every work must have an equally detailed history across all four dimensions.

Different statements, different basis

A MADE Record should show not only a conclusion, but what it is based on.

What the creator says

Information provided by the creator about the work and process.

What connects to available materials

Statements linked to drafts, files, versions, AI chats, notes, or other available materials.

What can be observed directly

Information visible in files or the available work history.

What follows from analysis

Conclusions drawn from the available information.

What remains unknown or disputed

Information that is incomplete, uncertain, or cannot be established.

Missing material, by itself, does not mean that someone is trying to mislead others.

What a MADE Record does not do

More context. Not proof of authorship.

A MADE Record is not an AI detector, legal certificate, proof of authorship, proof of copyright or ownership, a guarantee that all process materials were preserved, or an automatic decision about who owns the creative contribution.

Its purpose is to make the human role, AI use, and available creation information easier to understand. Decisions about rights, acceptability, quality, or value are made separately.
