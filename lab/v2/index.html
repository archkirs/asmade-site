What AsMade creates

One work. One record of how it was made.

A MADE Record is linked to one specific work and brings the key context about how it was created into one place.

Who was involved?

The people and roles involved in creating the work.

What did the person do?

The actions, choices, edits, and decisions that shaped the result.

Where was AI used?

Where AI was used and how its output became part of the work.

What materials are available?

Available drafts, files, versions, AI chats, notes, and other process materials.

What remains unknown?

Information that is missing, uncertain, or cannot be established from what is available.

The missing context

The result is visible. The human contribution and the role of AI may not be.

Finished work

Shows the final result, but not always who made the key decisions or what role AI played.

AI chat

Shows part of the interaction with AI, but the work may also involve research, drafts, files, other tools, selection, editing, and decisions made outside the chat.

MADE Record

Brings the key context around one specific work into one place. It doesn't try to capture everything; it helps people understand how the work was created without having to piece the process together from scattered files and chats.

How it works

From one specific work to a clearer picture of how it was made.

1

Identify the specific work

Each MADE Record is linked to a defined work and version.

2

Review available information

Available drafts, files, versions, notes, AI chats, and other materials connected to the work may be reviewed.

3

Create the MADE Record

AsMade brings together what the person did, how AI was used, key actions and decisions, available materials, and important limitations.

4

The reader reviews and decides

The reader can start with the main picture, then explore the process and available materials in more detail when needed. The final judgment remains theirs.

More about the structure

MADE helps organise information about how the work was created.

MADE offers four ways to look at how the work was created.

MADEWhat it covers
MaterialsThe work itself and available drafts, files, notes, sources, versions, AI chats, and other materials
ActionsResearching, creating, using AI, selecting, editing, checking, and revising
DecisionsWhat was accepted, changed, rejected, or approved, and how those choices shaped the result
EvolutionHow the work changed over time, when that history is available

MADE is not a rigid template with four required, equally detailed parts. It is a flexible way to organise information about how a work was created.

More context, not a ready-made verdict

Not every statement in a MADE Record is supported in the same way.

A MADE Record can distinguish, for example, between:

Creator-provided information

Information provided by the creator about the work and process.

Information linked to materials

Statements linked to drafts, files, versions, AI chats, notes, or other materials.

Findings from analysis

Conclusions drawn from the available information.

Unresolved questions

Information that remains incomplete, uncertain, or cannot be established from the available information.

AsMade does not reduce these differences to a simple “verified” label.

What AsMade does not do

More information. The decision remains yours.

A MADE Record is not an AI detector, a legal certificate, proof of copyright or ownership, proof that a work was created entirely by a person, or a guarantee that the content itself is true or correct.

AsMade helps people better understand how a work was created. It does not make the final decision for you.
