The review problem

The same “AI used” label can hide very different processes.

One person may use AI only for wording. Another may use it for source images but make the creative direction, selection and assembly decisions. Another may use AI across research, drafting, translation and revision. A binary disclosure alone does not explain those differences.

Without a RecordYou may have to infer context from the final result or reconstruct it from chats, drafts, screenshots and explanations.
With a MADE RecordYou can start from an organised account of the Work, contribution, support and uncertainty, then inspect deeper material selectively.

Four questions before you decide.

The Record is designed to support judgment without making that judgment itself. It does not collapse different kinds of evidence or uncertainty into a single “verified” label.

01 · Work

What exactly is being reviewed?

A specific work and version with a persistent Record identity.

02 · Contribution

Who did what?

Human actions, AI assistance, editing, selection and significant decisions can be distinguished.

03 · Support

What backs each important finding?

Statements can link to available Materials and Evidence Status while access remains separately controlled.

04 · Limits

What is missing or uncertain?

Gaps, restricted material and unresolved questions remain visible instead of being silently interpreted as certainty.
