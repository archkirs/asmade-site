The premise

The final file is the ending. The useful context came before it.

When AI is involved, the question is not only whether a tool appeared. A reader may need to understand who set direction, what the tool contributed, what was rejected, what changed, what was selected, and which parts of that account can actually be supported.

Without a RecordThe reader reconstructs context from the result, chats, screenshots, drafts, notes and explanations.
With a MADE RecordThe important context is organised around one identifiable Work and presented with its support and limits.

One Record. Four questions.

Not four equal boxes. Four reading moves that help a person move from the finished Work to the basis behind the Record without turning the page into a raw evidence dump.

01 · Work

What are we looking at?

A specific work and version. The Record is not a person-level score.

02 · Contribution

Who did what?

Human direction, AI assistance, editing, selection and other significant roles.

03 · Support

What backs the account?

Available materials and Evidence Status remain connected to individual findings.

04 · Limits

What do we still not know?

Gaps and uncertainty remain part of the Record instead of being silently filled in.

MADE can organise Materials, Actions, Decisions and Evolution where useful.The reader still makes the final judgment.

What a MADE Record is not

More context without pretending the context is a verdict.

Not an AI detectorNot legal certificationNot copyright or ownership proofNot proof of fully human authorshipNot a truth guarantee

AsMade records what can be said about one Work from the available information, keeps the basis and uncertainty visible, and leaves the final decision with the reader.
