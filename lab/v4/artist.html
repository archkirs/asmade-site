The creative gap

AI-generated material can hide the difference between generation and direction.

Two works can both contain AI-generated material while involving very different human roles. One may rely heavily on generated output with little change. Another may involve sustained direction, multiple rejected options, editing, compositing, text work, sequencing and manual assembly.

Direction

What was the creator trying to make?

Intent, scene goals, style choices and constraints can matter before generation begins.

Selection

What was kept, changed or rejected?

Selection and redirection can shape the final result even when AI generated source material.

Assembly

What became the finished work?

Editing, layout, combination, text and final approval can remain human actions around AI-assisted assets.

Make the creative role visible without turning it into a badge.

The Record can describe contribution, support and uncertainty. It does not automatically establish authorship, copyright, ownership, originality, quality or rights.

01 · Work

Anchor the Record to one output.

One defined creative work and version, not a blanket statement about the creator.

02 · Contribution

Make meaningful choices visible.

Direction, generation requests, rejection, selection, editing and assembly can be described separately.

03 · Support

Connect findings to materials.

Available process records can support specific claims while restricted or missing materials remain clearly marked.

04 · Limits

Do not fill the gaps.

Incomplete generation history or declaration-based steps remain visible limitations rather than being converted into certainty.
