About the work

The Count of Monte Cristo — Page 050 is a comic page created by Ian Kirs as part of an AI-assisted graphic adaptation project.

The finished page combines human creative direction, AI-assisted image generation and text development, and manual final assembly.

How it was made

Ian planned the page and directed the scenes, framing and visual choices.

AI tools were used to help generate panel images and develop parts of the scenes and wording.

The process was iterative. Different image options were generated, reviewed and rejected before the final selections were made. Ian chose the images used in the page and assembled the finished page in Clip Studio Paint EX.

Human contribution

  • planning the page and scene direction;
  • choosing framing and visual direction;
  • reviewing AI-generated alternatives;
  • rejecting unsuitable results;
  • selecting the final images;
  • assembling the finished page;
  • reviewing and correcting the Record.

AI assistance

  • generating image alternatives;
  • developing scene ideas and visual options;
  • assisting with wording and text development.

The Record describes the roles shown by the available process materials. It does not assume that using AI means that human contribution was either high or low.

How the work evolved

The available materials show that the page did not come from a single accepted AI output. Several image alternatives were considered. Some were rejected or replaced. Earlier visual versions and unused alternatives show changes before the final page was assembled.

Page planning→Generated options→Human review and rejection→Final selection→Page assembly

The complete generation and selection history is not available, so the Record does not claim to reconstruct every step.

What remains uncertain

Some parts of the process cannot be fully reconstructed from the available evidence. Not every referenced project material was inspected.

The exact source edition or translation used for the adapted text was not established in the submitted evidence.

This Record does not establish legal authorship, ownership or copyright, and it does not prove that the available evidence represents every step of creation.
