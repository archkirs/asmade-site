The problem

A final file does not show the work behind it.

A long AI chat is hard to review. A folder of drafts is also hard to read.

A short process record can make AI-assisted work easier to understand.

How it works

1

Prepare the work

Start with the final file, assignment, design, article, image, research note, or other work.

Work
2

Collect process materials

Add drafts, notes, AI chats, screenshots, versions, source files, or file history when available.

Materials
3

Create a short record

The process is summarized into human contribution, AI assistance, materials, and limits.

Record
4

Share it for review

A reviewer sees a clear summary instead of guessing from the final file alone.

Review

Human contribution
Shows planning, selection, editing, rejection, direction, and final approval.

AI assistance
Explains where AI helped and what role it played.

Process materials
Organizes drafts, notes, AI chats, screenshots, versions, and sources.

Clear limits
States what the record can show and what it cannot prove.

Choose your use case

Different people need the same basic record.

The core idea is simple: show the work behind AI-assisted work. Each page explains one use case.

Use cases

Students Show your teacher how the work was made. Artists Show the human process behind creative work. Reviewers Ask for a clear record instead of guessing.

What the record can show

The record is a readable summary. It is not a judge, detector, certificate, or legal claim.

Possible fields

Human contribution what the person planned, chose, edited, or approved
AI assistance how AI helped in the process
Drafts and notes materials that support the process summary
Limits of the record what still needs human review

FAQ

A record helps review. It does not replace review.

The pilot record is based on materials provided by the person who made the work. It does not independently decide originality, ownership, academic acceptance, platform compliance, or quality.

In the current pilot, records are created manually. Do not submit confidential materials until invited to a controlled pilot.
