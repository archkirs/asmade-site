The operator determines why and how personal information is used for the AsMade public site and pilot application and acts as the data controller for that processing.

2. Information handled when you visit the site

The AsMade site is hosted by Vercel. When your browser requests a page or asset, Vercel may process technical information needed to deliver, protect, and diagnose the site, such as:

  • IP address;
  • approximate location derived from the IP address;
  • requested page or asset;
  • date and time;
  • browser, device, operating system, language, network, and referrer information;
  • diagnostic, performance, and security information.

AsMade has not intentionally enabled a separate analytics or advertising service and does not maintain a separate visitor-profile database.

3. Information collected through the pilot application

The pilot application is hosted by Tally and may collect:

  • the landing-page source: general, student, artist, or reviewer;
  • the kind of work you may want to document;
  • why you may need a MADE Record;
  • who may review it;
  • whether AsMade may contact you;
  • your email address when you select Yes or Maybe for follow-up contact.

No work files are uploaded through the current application form.

Do not enter confidential, sensitive, legal, medical, financial, school, client, credential, or unnecessary third-party information.

For application responses, AsMade is the controller and Tally provides the form service as a processor.

4. Information you send by email

If you email hello@useasmade.com, AsMade may process your email address and display name, the subject and content of your message, message headers and delivery information, and information you choose to include in later correspondence.

Incoming project mail is forwarded through Porkbun and stored in authorised Gmail accounts. Outgoing replies may be delivered through Resend.

Do not send work files, confidential materials, or sensitive personal information unless AsMade has first provided a separate approved submission route and notice.

5. Why we use information

We use personal information to:

  • deliver, protect, and troubleshoot the public site;
  • understand which public landing page led to an application;
  • assess voluntary pilot applications;
  • contact applicants who selected Yes or Maybe and provided an email address;
  • answer direct questions;
  • prevent abuse and protect the site, form, and communication systems;
  • comply with applicable legal obligations and respond to lawful requests.

The working legal basis is the operator's legitimate interest in operating and protecting the public site and evaluating voluntary pilot applications, together with the applicant's request or indicated permission for pilot follow-up. Where applicable law requires another basis, the operator will use that basis and update this Policy.

You may ask AsMade to stop contacting you about the pilot at any time.

6. Who may receive information

Personal information may be available to:

  • Ian Kirs where needed to administer the site, application, and correspondence;
  • Daria Kirs where approved access is implemented and needed for project coordination;
  • Vercel for site hosting and technical delivery;
  • Tally for the pilot application and response storage;
  • Porkbun for domain and incoming-email forwarding services;
  • Google for Gmail account infrastructure and correspondence storage;
  • Resend for outgoing email delivery and related delivery information;
  • professional advisers or public authorities where disclosure is legally required or reasonably necessary to protect rights and security.

AsMade does not sell application information and does not use it for third-party advertising.

7. International processing

Some service providers may process information in countries outside Serbia or make it available to their authorised subprocessors under their own service terms and safeguards.

Tally states that form data is encrypted in transit and at rest and stored in Europe. Vercel, Google, Porkbun, and Resend may operate internationally.

8. Retention

Public-site technical data

AsMade does not maintain a separate visitor analytics database. Technical hosting information is retained by Vercel according to the enabled service, account plan, and provider policies.

Pilot applications

Applications that do not become active pilot cases will be retained for no more than six months from submission and then manually deleted from Tally, unless a longer period is required by law or needed to handle an active request or dispute.

Tally may retain deleted submissions in provider-controlled Trash, backups, or other systems under its service rules. AsMade's monthly review also removes the relevant submissions from the ordinary Tally Trash interface after confirming that they should no longer be retained there.

If an applicant becomes a selected participant, later project materials and participant information will be governed by a separate participant notice and approved handling process.

Email correspondence

Email is retained only while needed to answer the message, manage the relevant pilot relationship, protect rights, or meet legal obligations. A separate fixed retention rule must be adopted before email is used for participant-material collection.

9. Your choices and rights

Depending on the applicable law and circumstances, you may ask AsMade to:

  • confirm whether personal information about you is being processed;
  • provide access to or a copy of that information;
  • correct inaccurate information;
  • delete information that is no longer needed or is processed without a valid basis;
  • restrict or object to certain processing;
  • stop pilot follow-up messages;
  • explain the source, purpose, and recipients of your information.

Send requests to hello@useasmade.com. AsMade may need enough information to verify that the request relates to you, but will not ask for unnecessary identity documents.

You may also contact the Commissioner for Information of Public Importance and Personal Data Protection of the Republic of Serbia about personal-data concerns.

10. Adults only for the current pilot

The current planned pilot is limited to people aged 18 or older. The public site is not designed to collect personal information from children.

If you believe a child has submitted personal information, contact hello@useasmade.com.

11. Cookies and similar technologies

AsMade does not intentionally set first-party analytics or advertising cookies on the current static site.

The site links to Tally as an external service. When you open Tally, its own cookies, privacy notice, and service settings apply.

No cookie-consent banner is planned for the current AsMade site unless a live check or later feature shows that non-essential cookies or similar tracking technologies are used.

12. Security

AsMade uses a limited current architecture and restricts access to the people and providers needed for the relevant task. No online system is completely secure, and this Policy does not guarantee absolute security.

Do not use the public application or email to send confidential or sensitive material.

13. Third-party links

The site links to Tally, professional profiles, and other third-party services. Their privacy practices are governed by their own notices and settings.

14. Changes to this Policy

This Policy may change when the public flow, service providers, or pilot process changes. The effective date and last-updated date will be shown at the top.

A materially new processing activity, such as a Contact form, file upload, participant-material workflow, or public record archive, must be described before or when it becomes active.
