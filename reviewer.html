The review problem

A final file shows too little. A long AI chat shows too much.

The final result may not make the human contribution and AI role clear. A raw AI conversation may contain too much detail while still missing work done in other tools, files, drafts, and decisions.

A MADE Record brings the useful context together in one place so you can start with the main picture and go deeper only when needed.

How to read a MADE Record

Start with the result. Open the deeper layers when you need them.

Record

See the work, creator information, main findings, and important limitations first.

Process

Review the main stages of creation and the roles of the person and AI.

Evidence

See what available materials relate to individual findings and what can actually be accessed.

History keeps the Record version and meaningful changes visible without making version tracking the first thing you have to read.

How it works

From one specific work to a clearer basis for your own evaluation.

1

Identify the work

The MADE Record is linked to a specific work and version.

2

Review available information

Files, drafts, versions, notes, AI chats, and other relevant materials may be considered when available.

3

Create the MADE Record

AsMade organises the human and AI roles, important decisions, available support, and significant limits.

4

You make the judgment

Use the Record as additional context. Quality, acceptability, truth, value, and other decisions remain yours or your organisation’s.

Different information, different basis

Not every statement in a MADE Record is supported in the same way.

Creator-provided information

Some information comes from the creator’s own account of the work and process.

Available supporting information

Some statements connect to files, drafts, versions, screenshots, AI chats, or other available materials.

Open or uncertain questions

Some questions remain incomplete, disputed, unsupported, or outside what the Record can decide.

A MADE Record should make these differences visible rather than hiding them behind a simple “verified” label.

What materials mean

A material can add context. It does not decide the case by itself.

A file may support one action. An AI chat may show one interaction. A document version may show one change.

No single item necessarily explains the whole process, and the absence of one item does not automatically mean misconduct, deception, or bad faith.

Limits

More context. Not a ready-made verdict.

A MADE Record can help make the origin and creation of a work easier to understand. It does not decide quality, acceptability, truth, authorship, ownership, or legal rights for you.
