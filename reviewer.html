AsMade for Reviewers

Understand how the work was made before making your own judgment.

A finished work shows the result. But the result alone doesn't always show what the person did, where AI was used, or which decisions shaped the work.

A MADE Record brings that context together for one specific work. It shows what the person did, how AI was used, the actions and decisions that shaped the work, what information is available about the process, and what remains unknown.

iA MADE Record gives you more context for your evaluation. It does not make the decision for you.

The result is visible. How it was made may not be.

Knowing that AI was used tells you very little on its own.

In one work, AI may be used only to check text. In another, to generate source images. In a third, for research, translation, or a first draft.

At the same time, the person may still have made the key decisions, selected what to use, edited the result, and checked the final work. The finished result does not always make that difference clear.

A MADE Record helps show not just that AI was used, but how it fit into the process of creating one specific work.

What a MADE Record can show

The main context, in one place.

What is this work?

The specific work and version the Record is connected to.

Who was involved?

The people or roles identified as involved in creating the work.

What did the person do?

The actions and decisions connected to the result.

Where was AI used?

Where AI was used and how its output became part of the work.

What materials are available?

The available materials connected to the main statements about how the work was created.

What remains unknown?

Information that is missing, uncertain, incomplete, or disputed.

Different sources show different parts of the work

An AI chat can show part of the process, not the whole creation history.

The work may also involve research, drafts, source files, other software, alternative versions, selection, editing, checking, and decisions made outside the AI chat.

That is why even a long AI conversation may show only part of how the work was created. A MADE Record brings the key information around one specific result into one place.

How it works

From one specific work to clearer context for your evaluation.

1

Identify the specific work

Each MADE Record is linked to a defined work and version.

2

Review available information

Available files, versions, drafts, notes, AI chats, and other materials connected to the work may be reviewed.

3

Create the MADE Record

AsMade brings together what the person did, how AI was used, key actions and decisions, available materials, and important limitations.

4

You review the Record

Start with the main picture and move to more detailed information when needed.

5

You make your own decision

The MADE Record adds context. It does not decide quality, acceptability, truth, or value for you.

More context, not a ready-made verdict

Not every statement in a MADE Record is supported in the same way.

A MADE Record can distinguish between:

Creator-provided information

Information provided by the creator about the work and process.

Information linked to materials

Statements linked to files, versions, drafts, AI chats, notes, or other available materials.

Directly observable information

Information visible in files or the available work history.

Findings from analysis

Conclusions drawn from the available information.

Unresolved questions

Questions that remain unanswered, uncertain, incomplete, or disputed.

AsMade does not reduce these differences to a simple “verified” label.

Materials add context, not a verdict

One file, chat, or version may explain only part of the process.

An available file may support one action or stage. An AI chat may show one interaction with the model. A document version may show one change.

But no single material necessarily explains the whole process. And the absence of a particular material does not, by itself, mean misconduct, deception, or bad faith.

A MADE Record helps show what is known, what information supports that understanding, and where limitations remain.

Context for your own decision

One Record can be useful in different situations.

A MADE Record may be useful to teachers, clients, editors, publishers, employers, contest organisers, platforms, professional communities, and other people or organisations that need clearer context about how a specific work was created.

A MADE Record does not replace their own rules, criteria, or procedures.

What a MADE Record does not do

More context. Not a ready-made verdict.

A MADE Record is not an AI detector or a legal certificate. It does not prove authorship, copyright, or ownership, guarantee that every material was provided, guarantee that the content itself is true, or decide the quality or acceptability of the work.

AsMade helps make it easier to understand how a work was created. The final evaluation remains with the person or organisation using the Record.
