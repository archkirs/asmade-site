AsMade for students

Used AI in an assignment? Show what you did.

The finished assignment shows the result. But it may not show what the student did, where AI was used, or which decisions were made by the person.

A MADE Record adds that context around one specific assignment: the student role, AI use, important steps and decisions, available process information, and what remains unknown.

Why this can matter

Using AI does not, by itself, explain how the assignment was done.

One student may use AI only for grammar. Another may use it to explore ideas. Another may start from AI-generated text and then rewrite it extensively.

The finished assignment may look similar from the outside. A MADE Record helps make the actual student and AI roles clearer.

What a MADE Record can show

More than the result — without asking a teacher to reconstruct the whole process.

QuestionWhat the Record can clarify
What was the task?The assignment context and the specific work or version being discussed
What did the student do?Research, writing, checking, selecting, editing, revising, and other human work
Where was AI used?The tasks AI supported and how AI output entered or influenced the assignment
What supports the account?Available sources, drafts, versions, notes, AI chats, and other relevant materials
What is still unclear?Missing, incomplete, or uncertain parts of the process

Different sources answer different questions

A final assignment and an AI chat do not tell the same story.

The final assignment shows what was submitted, but not always the student contribution or the role of AI.

An AI chat may show part of the interaction with AI, but not necessarily research, source work, editing, other tools, or decisions made outside the chat.

A MADE Record connects the important context around the assignment in one place.

Simple example

The same AI tool can be used in very different ways.

The student may do

Choose the topic, read sources, form the main argument, write the first draft, check facts, and make the final decisions.

AI may help with

Alternative wording, grammar checks, translation of selected passages, or discussion of structure.

The teacher still decides

A MADE Record can show the difference, but it does not decide whether that AI use was permitted under the relevant course rules.

How it works

From one assignment to a clearer account of how it was done.

1

Identify the assignment

The Record is linked to a specific assignment and version.

2

Review available information

Drafts, sources, notes, versions, AI chats, and other relevant materials may be considered when available.

3

Create and check the Record

AsMade organises the student role, AI use, decisions, available support, and limits. The student can check factual information about their own work.

4

The teacher makes the judgment

The MADE Record adds context. The final decision remains with the teacher or institution.

The MADE structure

A deeper way to organise information about the work.

MADEStudent-work context
MaterialsThe assignment, final work, drafts, sources, notes, versions, AI chats, and other available materials
ActionsResearching, writing, using AI, checking, editing, and revising
DecisionsChoices about sources, structure, content, and how AI output was used or rejected
EvolutionHow the assignment changed from early work to the final version, when that history is available

Limits

More context. The decision still belongs to the teacher.

Missing process material does not automatically mean misconduct or deception. A MADE Record should make clear what is available, what is supported, and what remains uncertain.
