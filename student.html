Context that is not in the finished work

Using AI doesn't, by itself, explain how the work was done.

One student may use AI to check grammar. Another may use it to brainstorm ideas. A third may use it to produce a first draft that is later completely reworked. Another may use AI across several stages of the process.

The finished work doesn't always make those differences visible. A MADE Record helps show how AI was used in a specific assignment and what the student did.

What this can look like

AI can play very different roles in student work.

Imagine a student writing an essay.

The student does

Chooses the topic, finds and reads sources, forms the main argument, writes the first draft, checks facts, and makes the final decisions.

AI helps with

Alternative wording, grammar checks, translation of selected passages, and discussion of structure.

Course rules still apply

A MADE Record can make these roles clearer. It does not decide whether the AI use was allowed under the course rules. That remains with the teacher, instructor, or institution.

Your role in the work

Make your role in the work easier to see.

What was assigned?

The task you were given and the result you submitted.

What did you do?

What you researched, wrote, checked, selected, edited, or changed.

Where was AI used?

Where AI was used and how its output became part of the work.

Which decisions did you make?

The sources, wording, ideas, or options you selected, changed, or rejected.

How did the work evolve?

The drafts, versions, and changes from the beginning of the work to the final result.

What materials are available?

Available sources, drafts, versions, AI chats, notes, and other relevant materials.

What remains unknown?

Parts of the process that cannot be established from the available information.

How it works

From one assignment to a clearer picture of how the work was done.

1

Identify the specific work

Each MADE Record is linked to a defined assignment and version.

2

Review available information

Available drafts, sources, notes, versions, AI chats, and other materials connected to the assignment may be reviewed.

3

Create the MADE Record

AsMade brings together what the student did, how AI was used, key decisions, available materials, and important limitations.

4

The student reviews their information

The student can review their statements and other factual details before the Record is shared.

5

The teacher or instructor makes the final judgment

The MADE Record adds context. The final decision remains with the teacher, instructor, or institution.

More about the structure

MADE helps organise information about how the work was done.

MADEWhat it covers
MaterialsThe assignment, final work, drafts, sources, notes, versions, AI chats, and other available materials
ActionsResearching, writing, using AI, checking, editing, and revising
DecisionsChoices about sources, structure, content, and how AI output was used or not used
EvolutionHow the work changed from the first stages to the final version, when that history is available

MADE is not a rigid template with four required, equally detailed parts. It is a flexible way to organise information about how the work was done.

More context, not a ready-made verdict

Not every statement in a MADE Record is supported in the same way.

A MADE Record can distinguish between:

Student-provided information

Information provided by the student about their own work and process.

Information linked to materials

Statements linked to drafts, sources, versions, AI chats, notes, or other available materials.

Directly observable information

Information visible in files or the available work history.

Findings from analysis

Conclusions drawn from the available information.

Unresolved questions

Questions that cannot be answered from the available information.

Missing material does not, by itself, mean misconduct or deception.

What a MADE Record does not do

More context. The decision remains with the teacher.

A MADE Record is not an AI detector or a cheating-detection system. It does not prove independent work or originality, decide whether AI use was allowed, or assign a grade.

It also does not replace school, university, or course rules. Its purpose is to make it easier to understand how the work was done.
