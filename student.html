AsMade for Students

Used AI in an assignment? Show what you did.

The finished work shows the result. But it does not always make clear which parts the student did, where AI was used, or which decisions were made by the person.

A MADE Record brings that context together around one specific assignment: the student role, AI use, important stages, and available process information.

Context that is not in the finished work

Using AI does not, by itself, explain how the work was done.

One student may use AI to check grammar. Another may use it to find ideas. A third may use it to produce a first draft that is then completely reworked. Someone else may use AI much more deeply.

The finished work does not always make those differences visible. A MADE Record helps show how AI was actually used in a specific assignment and what the student did.

Your role in the work

Show not only the result, but your work on it.

What was assigned?

The task the student was given and the result that was submitted.

What did the student do?

What they researched, wrote, checked, selected, edited, or changed themselves.

Where was AI used?

Which tasks AI supported and how its output affected the work.

Which decisions did the person make?

Which sources, wording, ideas, or options were selected, changed, or rejected.

How did the work evolve?

The drafts, versions, and changes between the beginning of the work and the final result.

What is the account based on?

Available sources, drafts, versions, AI chats, notes, and other relevant materials.

What remains unknown?

Parts of the process that cannot be established from the available information.

What is the difference?

Different materials answer different questions.

Finished work

Shows the result, but does not always make the student contribution and the role of AI clear.

AI chat

Shows part of the interaction with AI, but not necessarily research, source work, editing, other tools, or decisions made outside the chat.

MADE Record

Connects the important information around one specific work and helps show the overall picture without asking the teacher to reconstruct it from every file and a long AI-chat history.

What this can look like

The same AI can be used in completely different ways.

Imagine a student writing an essay.

The student does

Chooses the topic, finds and reads sources, forms the main argument, writes the first draft, checks facts, and makes the final decisions.

AI helps with

Alternative wording, grammar checks, translation of selected passages, and discussion of structure.

The teacher still decides

A MADE Record can show this difference. It does not decide whether that use of AI was allowed under the course rules. That remains with the teacher or institution.

How it works

From a student assignment to a clearer account of the process.

1

Identify the specific work

The MADE Record is linked to a defined assignment and version.

2

Review available information

Drafts, sources, notes, versions, AI chats, and other materials connected to the assignment may be reviewed when available.

3

Create the MADE Record

AsMade brings together the main student actions, AI use, important decisions, available materials, and limitations.

4

The student checks their information

The student can check their own statements and factual context before the Record is shared with someone else.

5

The teacher makes their own judgment

The MADE Record adds context. The final decision remains with the teacher or institution.

More about the structure

MADE helps organise information about the work.

MADEStudent-work context
MaterialsThe assignment, final work, drafts, sources, notes, versions, AI chats, and other available materials
ActionsResearching, writing, using AI, checking, editing, and revising
DecisionsChoices about sources, structure, content, and how AI output was used or not used
EvolutionHow the work changed from the first stages to the final version, when that history is available

MADE does not mean that every work must have four equally detailed parts.

Different statements, different basis

The Record should show not only a conclusion, but what it is based on.

What the student says

Information provided by the student about their own work and process.

What connects to available materials

Statements linked to drafts, sources, versions, AI chats, notes, or other available materials.

What can be observed directly

Information visible in files or the available work history.

What follows from analysis

Conclusions drawn from the available information.

What remains unknown

Questions that cannot be established from the available information.

Missing material, by itself, does not mean misconduct or deception.

What a MADE Record does not do

More context. The decision remains with the teacher.

A MADE Record is not an AI detector, cheating-detection system, automatic proof of independent work, proof of originality, a decision about whether AI use was allowed, or a grading system.

It also does not replace the rules of a school, university, or individual course. Its purpose is to make the creation process easier to understand.
